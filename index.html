Queens Has the Highest Percentage of "Poor Streets" in the City, According to New NYC Department of Transporation Report

In a new report, NYC DOT officials rate streets across the five boroughs. From these ratings, Queens accounted for the majority of streets labeled as "poor".

By Rachel Goldman

Nov. 23, 2022

Queens has the most "poor" street conditions across the five boroughs according to a report from the New York Department of Transportation (NYC DOT) This report is an assessment of the quality of different street segments across the five boroughs. With 687 "poor streets", streets in Queens make up more than 39% of all "poor streets".

Additionally, Queens had the highest number of 311 requests related to street conditions in the past eight days.

Despite the number of 311 calls, DOT Commissioner Ydanis Rodriguez announced earlier this month that DOT had surpassed the number of intersections they vowed to improve earlier in the year. "In January, we made a commitment: We declared intersections ‘sacred spaces’ and pledged to make safety enhancements at 1,000 of them — and today, we can proudly say both that we exceeded that pledge and that we are not done yet," said Commissioner Rodriguez.
